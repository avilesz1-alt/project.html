When AI-Generated Art Enters the Market
Site Author
 Stanford Gradute School of Business


Abstract
 This site offers a new perspective on how computer-generated art can impact an image-based online platform, and what it means for the business owners, consumers, and human artists. It first begins by bringing up a case in 2024 where the New York Times sued the company, OpenAI, for feeding their content to algorithms without permission or prior compensation. The lawsuit then raises the question of whether A.I. is overshadowing the human products its trained on. To get to the bottom of this question, two professors investigate the impact of non-human-generated images. Through talks of it benefiting the consumers and producers, and not actual human creatives, the site concludes that human input being left out of the equation is concerning.

 The Impact of A.I. On The Art World
Site Author
 Forbes
Abstract
 While shorter than the last source, this site goes over the pros and cons of including A.I. in the art world, as well as the market for buying/selling. The first 3 excerpts of information bring the readers attention to how much the COVID-19 pandemic affected not only businesses around the globe but also the art market. Those years of isolation would eventually lead to new and innovative ways to display works of art, such as online viewing rooms, remote auctions, and augmented reality simulations. Unbeknownst to the masses, this would also bring forth the use of A.I. in creative fields. Timeline aside, the site then goes over the benefits and drawbacks of utilizing this untapped form of assistance. For example, it can process information at a much faster speed, but theres uncertainty whether or not its predictions on auction prices are accurate enough to keep up with the fluidity of the art world.
